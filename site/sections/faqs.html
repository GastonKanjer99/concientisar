Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers about sensors, layers, indicators, and how to read the maps.

Which bands/sensors do we use and why?

C-band radar (Sentinel-1: VV, VH) to observe water/smooth surfaces (VV) and vegetation/moisture (VH). L-band radar (ALOS-2: HH, HV) penetrates canopy and measures woody structure/biomass (HV). Optical (Sentinel-2) provides NDVI and dNBR.

Why do we show those two example comparisons?

We use C-band (VH) for rapid vegetation and moisture changes, and L-band (HV) for deep structural damage. Together they give a more robust reading: surface + structure.

How many bands/polarizations and indices do we use?

What is biomass and what exactly do we show?

Biomass = living organic matter (trunks, branches, leaves). We show relative changes: L-band (HV) 2020 vs 2023 for woody structure, and C-band/NDVI for green cover/moisture.

Were native species lost?

Satellite detects cover/structure, not species. Signs of forest loss: high dNBR, persistent drop in L-band (HV) and low NDVI. Confirmation requires field and very high-resolution data.
